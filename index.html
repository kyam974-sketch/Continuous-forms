1 — Present Continuous: I am doing
Example
"I am reading a book right now."
Sto leggendo un libro adesso.
Use it for: something happening right now, at this moment.
Form: am / is / are + verb -ing
It is 3pm. Alex is at the office desk. Which sentence describes what is happening right now?
Sam is in the middle of an English lesson. Which sentence is correct?
2 — Past Continuous: I was doing
Example
"I was cooking dinner when the phone rang."
Stavo cucinando quando il telefono ha squillato.
Use it for: something that was in progress at a specific past moment — often interrupted by another action.
Form: was / were + verb -ing
Yesterday at 9pm, Maya was at home watching TV. Which sentence describes that moment?
Tom and Lena were in a meeting when an unexpected guest arrived. Which sentence is correct?
3 — Present Perfect Simple: I have done
Example
"I have finished the report."
Ho finito il report. (il risultato è qui, adesso)
Use it for: a finished action whose result matters now.
Form: have / has + past participle
Jordan just finished a project and wants to tell a colleague. Which is correct?
Lena has watched a series and wants to recommend it to a friend. Which is correct?
4 — Present Perfect Continuous: I have been doing
Example
"I have been working hard all day."
Ho lavorato duramente tutto il giorno — e sto ancora lavorando, o l'ho appena finito.
Use it for: an action that started in the past and is still going (or just stopped) — focus on duration and process.
Form: have / has + been + verb -ing
Sam started learning English a few months ago and is still learning. Sam wants to describe this. Which is most natural?
Alex started reading a book this morning and it is now evening — still reading. Which sentence fits?
5 — For vs Since
The difference
for + duration → how long?
"I have been learning English for six months." — per sei mesi
since + starting point → since when?
"I have been learning English since January." — da gennaio
for → a period of time: six months, two years, a long time, ages
since → a point in time: January, 2023, last year, I was a child
Maya started a new job in March. Choose:
"I have been working here _____ March."
Tom has lived in the same city for twenty years. Choose:
"I have been living here _____ twenty years."
A group of researchers has been studying the same problem for decades. Choose:
"Researchers have been working on this _____ decades."
Jordan and Sam have been friends since childhood. Choose:
"We have been friends _____ we were children."
6 — Putting it all together
Quick reminder
I am doing → right now, this moment
I was doing → in progress at a specific past moment
I have done → finished, result matters now
I have been doing → started in past, still going — focus on duration
Lena started a course last month and is still attending. She tells a friend. Which is correct?
Tom finished reading a book this morning. He is talking about it now. Which is correct?
Alex and Maya were in a meeting when their manager walked in. Which is correct?
Jordan has been learning a language for two years and feels more confident now. Complete:
"I _____ this language _____ two years and I feel much better now."
